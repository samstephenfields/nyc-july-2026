Central Park, Empire State & WC 3rd Place
🌳 Central Park 🏙 Empire State ⚽ 3rd Place Playoff
▾
🥐
Breakfast
Ess-a-Bagel
Legendary NYC bagel shop. Get a classic everything bagel with lox and cream cheese. There's a Midtown location on 3rd Ave — grab and go or sit in.
~$10–12 each

Morning
Central Park — South to North
Enter at 59th St (Columbus Circle). Stroll past Sheep Meadow, Bethesda Fountain & Terrace, The Lake, Strawberry Fields (John Lennon memorial), and Belvedere Castle. Allow 2–3 hours to wander.
Free
💡 Rent a rowboat on The Lake — $20 for 1 hour, very romantic
📸 Best shot: Bethesda Terrace from the steps above, framing the fountain and lake together. For something quieter, the Bow Bridge reflected in The Lake is one of the most photographed spots in New York.
🥗
Lunch
Picnic in the Park or Shake Shack (Madison Square Park)
Pick up sandwiches from a deli on the Upper West Side and eat on the grass, or head south to the original Shake Shack at Madison Square Park.
~$15/person
🏙
Afternoon
Empire State Building Observatory
Walk or subway down from Central Park to 34th St & 5th Ave. The main observation deck is on the 86th floor with open-air 360° views — one of the most iconic vantage points in the world. Book timed-entry tickets online in advance to skip the queue. The top-floor (102nd) deck costs more but the 86th is the classic experience.
86th floor: ~$44/person · 102nd floor add-on: ~$20 extra
💡 Book timed-entry at esbnyc.com — walk-up queues can be 2+ hours
📸 Best shot: from the deck, frame Central Park as a green rectangle stretching north between the buildings — uniquely recognisable. Also look south-west for the One World Trade tower. For a photo of the building itself, 34th St looking west gives the classic straight-on view.
⚽
Evening — World Cup 3rd Place Playoff
Watch the 3rd Place Match at a Football Bar
The FIFA World Cup 3rd place playoff takes place on July 18 — the day before the Final. Kick-off is typically 6pm ET. NYC will already be buzzing with fans from the finalist nations pouring into the city, making this a brilliant night to be watching football in a bar. Best spots:

Nevada Smiths (100 3rd Ave, East Village) — NYC's most famous football bar, a proper supporter's pub atmosphere with wall-to-wall screens.

Smithfield Hall (339 W 49th St, Midtown West) — massive multi-screen bar, one of the best in the city for a big match.

Rarely Ever Sober / Playwright Irish Pub (Midtown) — packed with travelling fans on World Cup nights, great atmosphere.

The build-up to the Final means the whole city feels like a carnival — even the walk between bars is an event.
Dinner + drinks at the bar: ~$50–70 for two
💡 Book a table at your chosen bar in advance — 3rd place night with the Final eve crowd means everywhere fills fast

9/11 Memorial, Chinatown & WC Final
🕊 9/11 Memorial 🥢 Chinatown Brooklyn Bridge ⚽ WC Final
▾
🥞
Breakfast
Clinton St. Baking Company
Famous for its pancakes and eggs benedict. Arrive early — there's often a queue on weekends, but it moves quickly. On the Lower East Side.
~$20–25 each
🕊
Morning — Timed Entry Required
9/11 Memorial & Museum
One of the most moving places in New York. The twin reflecting pools sit in the exact footprints of the Twin Towers, surrounded by the names of nearly 3,000 victims. The underground museum tells the full story of the day. Allow 2–3 hours. Book timed-entry tickets in advance at 911memorial.org — it can sell out.
Memorial: free · Museum: ~$30/person
💡 Book the first entry slot of the day — quieter, and you'll be finished by late morning
📸 Best shot: kneel low at the edge of one of the reflecting pools — the water cascading into the void with names engraved around the rim makes for a powerful, respectful image. The Survivor Tree (a pear tree that survived the collapse) is also worth photographing.
🥢
Lunch
Chinatown
A 15-minute walk north from the 9/11 Memorial. NYC's Chinatown is one of the most vibrant in the world. Head to Joe's Shanghai (9 Pell St) for soup dumplings, or Nom Wah Tea Parlor (13 Doyers St) for NYC's oldest dim sum — open since 1920, on a curved alleyway once called the "Bloody Angle".
~$15–20/person
🌉
Afternoon
Brooklyn Bridge Walk & DUMBO
Walk south from Chinatown to the Brooklyn Bridge entrance (10 mins) and cross on foot — about 45 mins. Explore DUMBO on the Brooklyn side: great views of the Manhattan skyline, Jane's Carousel, and independent boutiques.
Free
📸 Best shot: from DUMBO, walk to the corner of Washington St & Water St — the Brooklyn Bridge frames perfectly between two red-brick warehouses with Manhattan behind. One of the most iconic street-level photos in New York. Also get a shot mid-bridge looking back at the Manhattan skyline.
⚽
Afternoon / Evening — Destination TBC
FIFA World Cup Final Watch-Along
The 2026 World Cup Final takes place on July 19th at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey — right next door to NYC. Kick-off is likely 6pm ET. The atmosphere in Manhattan will be electric. Top spots to watch: Nevada Smiths (East Village — NYC's oldest football bar), Smithfield Hall (Midtown West), or any Irish bar in Midtown. Some venues will have large outdoor screens.
Drinks at bar: ~$30–50 for two
💡 Book a table at a sports bar in advance — they'll fill up fast for the Final

Subway to Coney Island
Take the Q or D train from Union Square/14th St to Coney Island–Stillwell Ave. About 50 minutes — a classic NYC experience watching the city give way to beachside Brooklyn.

Mid-morning to Afternoon
Coney Island Boardwalk & Luna Park
Walk the famous boardwalk, ride the Cyclone (a 1927 wooden roller coaster — genuinely thrilling), and explore Luna Park. The Wonder Wheel is iconic. The beach is a proper sandy beach — great for a swim in July.
Luna Park wristband: ~$40/person · Beach: free
💡 The Wonder Wheel gondola cars are a brilliant way to see the whole of Coney Island
📸 Best shot: stand on the boardwalk facing the Wonder Wheel with the beach stretching behind you — classic Coney Island. From the top of the Wonder Wheel you'll get a sweeping view of the whole coastline and Atlantic Ocean. Grab a Nathan's hot dog and get a photo with the original 1916 sign.

Lunch
Nathan's Famous
The original Nathan's Famous hot dog stand on Surf Avenue — established 1916, home of the famous 4th of July hot dog eating contest. A Coney Island essential. Get a hot dog and crinkle-cut fries.
~$10–15 for two
🏖
Afternoon
Beach & Boardwalk Games
Spend the afternoon on the beach — it's a proper Atlantic beach, great for swimming in July (water is warm). Walk the boardwalk all the way to Brighton Beach ("Little Odessa") for a different flavour of Brooklyn.
🌅
~8:15pm — Sunset Spot
Pier 45 — Hudson River Park
After the beach, head back into Manhattan and walk over to Pier 45 in Hudson River Park (Christopher St, West Village). NYC's best free sunset viewpoint: a wide open pier jutting into the Hudson, facing due west toward New Jersey. In July, sunset is around 8:20pm and the sky turns deep orange and pink over the water. Completely free, often breezy after a hot day, and far quieter than the High Line.
Free · ~10 mins from Xi'an Famous Foods
💡 Arrive 20 minutes before sunset and stay until the sky fades — the post-sunset "blue hour" is equally beautiful
📸 Best shot: face west from the end of the pier and shoot directly into the setting sun over the Hudson. Silhouette shots of each other against the orange sky work brilliantly here. Bring a wide-angle or use panorama mode to capture the full sweep of the river.
🥟
Dinner on the way back
Xi'an Famous Foods — East Village
Hand-pulled noodles and spicy lamb burgers from a celebrated small chain. The East Village location is the original. Cheap, delicious, and entirely unlike anything else in NYC.
~$15–20 for two

8:30am — Book in Advance
Statue of Liberty
Take the first ferry from Battery Park at 8:30am. Stick to Liberty Island only today (skip Ellis Island) — you need to be back at Battery Park by 10:30am to make the Governor's Island ferry in time for your 11am spa. Liberty Island alone is stunning: close-up views of the statue, the museum in the pedestal, and incredible Manhattan skyline views from the water.

Ticket types: Reserve (grounds + exterior) or Monument (pedestal access). Crown tickets sell out months ahead — book at statuecruises.com.
Reserve: ~$24/person · Monument: ~$32 · Crown: ~$32 (very limited)
💡 Take the first ferry and you'll be one of very few people on the island — magical before the crowds arrive
📸 Best shot: on the ferry approaching Liberty Island, position yourself at the bow for the statue growing larger against the Manhattan skyline behind. On the island, the low-angle shot looking straight up from the base makes her look monumental. Heading back, the skyline view from the water is exceptional.
⛴
~10:35am — Getting to the Spa
Ferry to Governor's Island
The Governor's Island ferry departs from the Battery Maritime Building — a 5-minute walk from the Statue of Liberty terminal. The crossing is just 7 minutes. You'll arrive at Governor's Island around 10:45am — perfect for your 11am spa check-in.
~$4 round trip
♨
11am–4pm — Pre-booked · Lunch included
QC New York — Spa & Wellness
A world-class Italian-style spa on Governor's Island with thermal pools, saunas, hammam, and outdoor baths with spectacular views of the Statue of Liberty and Manhattan skyline. Completely unique in NYC. Lunch is included — no need to eat beforehand.
💡 The outdoor pools with the Manhattan skyline framed behind the Statue of Liberty are the highlight — make time for these early
📸 Best shot: from the outdoor thermal pools, you can frame the Statue of Liberty and Manhattan skyline in the same shot — genuinely unique. The steam rising from the pools on cooler mornings adds a dreamy quality. Waterproof phone case recommended.
🚲
4pm onwards — Optional
Explore Governor's Island
Ferries back to Manhattan run until 7pm in summer, so you have time after the spa. Rent bikes and cycle the car-free perimeter — views of Lower Manhattan, Brooklyn, and the Statue of Liberty. The island also has art installations and hammocks.
Bike rental: ~$12/hour

A short walk from the Freehand. The Flatiron Building is one of NYC's most iconic structures. Madison Square Park has the original Shake Shack and is a lovely spot to sit in the sun.

SoHo & Last Wander
SoHo is NYC's best shopping neighbourhood — cast-iron architecture, independent boutiques, and flagship stores all mixed together. Last chance for souvenirs before heading to Queens.
🍕
Lunch
Eataly Flatiron
Right at the Flatiron Building (200 5th Ave) — an enormous Italian food hall spanning an entire city block. Browse the fishmonger, the cheese counter, the pasta bar, and the bakery, then sit down at one of several restaurants inside. Pizza, pasta, charcuterie boards, or a quick espresso and pastry at the counter. One of NYC's best food experiences.
~$20–30/person depending on what you order
💡 Grab something from the deli counter to eat at Madison Square Park next door for a cheaper option

Early Afternoon
McSorley's Old Ale House — East Village
New York's oldest Irish bar, open since 1854. Only two beers on the menu — light or dark — served in pairs. Sawdust on the floor, wishbones on the ceiling left by soldiers heading to WWI who never came back. Absolutely authentic. A proper pub afternoon before the baseball.
~$10 for two rounds · 15 East 7th St, East Village
💡 Cash only · Gets busy in the evening — go in the afternoon for a more relaxed experience
🎾
Afternoon — ~2:00pm
Flushing Meadows Corona Park & USTA Grounds
Take the 7 train from Midtown to Mets–Willets Point (~30 mins). You'll step out right into Flushing Meadows Corona Park — and you're already at Citi Field for later.

The Unisphere — the giant 12-storey stainless steel globe built for the 1964 World's Fair. One of NYC's most underrated iconic landmarks, usually far less crowded than Times Square.

USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center — the home of the US Open. The tournament won't be running in July but you can walk the outer grounds, see Arthur Ashe Stadium up close (the world's largest tennis stadium), and get a real sense of the scale of the place. Occasional off-season tours run — worth checking usta.com ahead of the trip.

Flushing food scene — one of the best eating destinations in the entire city. The Golden Shopping Mall basement (41-28 Main St) has hand-pulled noodles, lamb skewers, and dumplings for a few dollars each. A perfect pre-game meal.
Park: free · USTA grounds: free · Food in Flushing: ~$10–15/person
💡 The Unisphere is a 10-minute walk from the 7 train exit — Citi Field is visible the whole way
📸 Best shot: stand at the reflecting pool directly in front of the Unisphere and shoot upward — the 12-storey steel globe fills the frame dramatically. The fountains running in summer add great foreground interest. Arthur Ashe Stadium looming in the background of a Unisphere shot is a uniquely Queens composition.

The Mets have a confirmed home series against the LA Dodgers from July 24–26 — one of the biggest rivalries in baseball right now, and a World Series rematch atmosphere guaranteed. Citi Field is a great modern stadium with excellent sightlines from every seat.

You're already in Flushing from the afternoon — it's a 5-minute walk from the Unisphere to the stadium entrance. Gates open ~90 mins before first pitch so you can explore the concourses, grab a Shake Shack (yes, inside the stadium) and find your seats at leisure.

Note: The Yankees are away in Philadelphia on July 24th.
Tickets: $30–90/person · Hot dogs & beer at the stadium: ~$40 for two
💡 Buy tickets now at mlb.com — Dodgers series will sell fast
📸 Best shot: arrive early and get a photo from your seats with the floodlit field stretching out below — baseball stadiums are photogenic in a way that's hard to replicate. The view from the upper concourse looking down the first-base line with Manhattan visible in the distance on a clear evening is stunning.

Good to Know
🚇
Subway — Use OMNY (contactless card or phone). $2.90/ride. Runs 24/7. Get a map at any station.
💵
Tipping — 20% at restaurants is standard. Pre-tip is built into card machines — select a percentage or enter custom.
📱
Apps — Google Maps for navigation, Resy/OpenTable for restaurant bookings, MLB app for game tickets.
🎫
Queues — Popular spots like Katz's, Prune, and Joe's Pizza have queues. Go early or arrive off-peak.
🚣
Active option — Free kayaking — Hudson River Park runs free community kayaking from Pier 84 (W 44th St) on weekends. No booking needed, just show up. Paddling with the Manhattan skyline behind you is genuinely special.
🚴
Active option — Citi Bike — Rent bikes and ride the Hudson River Greenway, 11 miles of car-free cycling along Manhattan's west side. Day pass ~$15 each via the Citi Bike app.
